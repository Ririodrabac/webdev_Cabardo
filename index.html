Welcome to the Province of Zamboanga del Sur

Zamboanga del Sur

Zamboanga del Sur is a province in the Philippines, shaped by indigenous groups, Spanish colonization, and subsequent development. Originally inhabited by Subanen people, the area also saw settlements of various Muslim groups and later migrants, particularly after World War II. The province was officially established in 1952, when it was separated from the larger Zamboanga province.

Early History and Indigenous Roots

Subanen Presence

The Subanen people, meaning "River Folk" were the earliest known inhabitants, settling along riverbanks and primarily engaged in agriculture.

Muslim Influence

Various Muslim groups, including the Tausūg, Sama-Bajau, and Yakan, also settled in the region, particularly along the coasts.

Chinese Records

A polity named "Sanmalan" in Chinese records from the 11th century is tentatively identified with Zamboanga, suggesting possible pre-Spanish connections.

Popular Tourist Spots

Dao-Dao Island

Pagadian City

A tranquil island getaway just a short boat ride from the city, known for its peaceful beaches and crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

Fort Militar

Tukuran

These ruins of a Spanish fort offer a glimpse into the area's colonial past. Located on a hill, it provides scenic views of the surrounding landscape and Illana Bay.

Lake Dasay

Lakewood

A beautiful mountain lake where visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, and hiking in the lush, natural surroundings. It's a serene escape from city life.

Blasan Falls

Ramon Magsaysay

This cascading waterfall is a stunning natural attraction, offering a refreshing retreat and a perfect spot for outdoor activities like rock climbing and rappelling.

Manga Falls

Molave

A picturesque, two-layered waterfall surrounded by lush trees and home to unique wildlife. It's a great spot for nature lovers and a cool place to relax and enjoy the scenery.
